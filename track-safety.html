Track Safety

Keeping the race safe, smooth, and professional

Step 1: Flag Signals

Flags are how we communicate with drivers. Every marshal must understand and use these correctly:

  • Green: Track is clear, race can begin or resume.
  • Yellow: Slow down, caution. An incident may be ahead.
  • Red: Stop immediately. Serious incident on track.
  • Blue: Let faster drivers pass safely.
  • Black: Driver disqualified. Return to pits immediately.

Always raise the flag fully and clearly, and maintain eye contact with drivers when possible.

Step 2: Marshal Duties

Marshals are responsible for watching the track and ensuring everyone is following the rules. Here's what you must do:

  • Stay alert and keep your eyes on the track at all times.
  • Use radios to report issues to race control immediately.
  • Do NOT enter the track unless the red flag is shown and all karts are stopped.
  • Support drivers in trouble by flagging and calling for assistance.

Step 3: Dangerous Driving

Our zero-tolerance policy includes:

  • No bumping or crashing other karts.
  • No reckless weaving or unsafe overtaking.
  • No driving off-track or stopping without cause.

If you see dangerous driving, raise the black flag and report the driver to race control.
